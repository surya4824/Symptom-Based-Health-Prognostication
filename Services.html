Symptoms

Symptoms serve as the body's language, communicating its distress signals when something isn't quite right. They are the whispers of imbalance, the subtle alarms that prompt us to pay attention to our health. Symptoms can range from the familiar, like a fever or cough, to the more obscure, such as tingling sensations or unexplained fatigue.
